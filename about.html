About

Yale University is a private Ivy League research university in New Haven, Connecticut. Founded in 1701, it is the third-oldest institution of higher learning in the United States and one of the nine colonial colleges chartered before the American Revolution. Yale is a highly selective university, with an acceptance rate of just over 6%. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, including the Yale College liberal arts program, the Yale Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, and the Yale Law School. Yale is home to many world-renowned scholars and researchers, and its alumni include many notable figures in business, politics, and the arts. The university is also home to a number of cultural institutions, including the Yale University Art Gallery, the Yale Peabody Museum of Natural History, and the Yale University Library.
